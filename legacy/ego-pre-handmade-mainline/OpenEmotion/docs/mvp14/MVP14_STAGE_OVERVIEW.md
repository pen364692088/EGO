# MVP14 — Endogenous Drives + Self-Maintenance

## 1. Purpose

MVP14 introduces endogenous drives and self-maintenance behavior.

The objective is not to produce more expressive self-descriptions.
The objective is to allow internal state pressures, tensions, and
maintenance needs to influence internal prioritization and behavior
inside governed boundaries.

## 2. Core Problem

Before MVP14, the system may maintain a persistent self-model,
but that model does not yet act as a true source of pressure.

Without endogenous drives:
- priorities remain externally dominated
- internal tensions do not reliably shape behavior
- long-horizon persistence remains shallow
- self-maintenance is reactive or absent

## 3. Scope

MVP14 focuses on:
- endogenous drive representation
- drive accumulation and decay
- homeostatic deviation detection
- self-maintenance routines
- drive competition and prioritization
- governed behavior influence

MVP14 does NOT yet focus on:
- full reflective self-analysis
- counterfactual self-evaluation
- open-ended self-directed developmental expansion

## 4. Architectural Position

Layer 1 — Governance Shell
Layer 2 — Proto-Self / SRAP
Layer 3 — Developmental Core Sandbox
Layer 4 — Persistent Self-Model
Layer 5 — Endogenous Drives + Self-Maintenance

## 5. Mandatory Principles

- drives must be represented structurally, not rhetorically
- self-maintenance must be traceable and replayable
- no drive may bypass governance
- internal pressure must affect prioritization through explicit channels
- homeostasis must be measurable
