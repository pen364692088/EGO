# MVP15 — Reflective Self / Counterfactual Self

## 1. Purpose

MVP15 introduces structured self-reflection and counterfactual
self-evaluation.

The objective is not to generate introspective prose.
The objective is to enable the system to examine its own states,
decisions, tendencies, and developmental paths as explicit objects
of analysis.

## 2. Core Problem

Before MVP15, the system may maintain a persistent self-model
and endogenous drives, but it still lacks a robust mechanism for
evaluating itself as a causal system.

Without reflection:
- internal patterns remain insufficiently examined
- recurring bias or error modes are harder to correct
- self-model revisions may remain shallow
- the system cannot compare actual vs alternative self-trajectories

## 3. Scope

MVP15 focuses on:
- reflective analysis of self-state and behavior
- counterfactual self-evaluation
- self-bias and self-error diagnosis
- reflective policy revision proposals
- replay-linked reflection artifacts

MVP15 does NOT yet focus on:
- unrestricted open-ended self-development
- unconstrained self-redefinition
- governance bypass through reflection authority

## 4. Architectural Position

Layer 1 — Governance Shell
Layer 2 — Proto-Self / SRAP
Layer 3 — Developmental Core Sandbox
Layer 4 — Persistent Self-Model
Layer 5 — Endogenous Drives + Self-Maintenance
Layer 6 — Reflective Self / Counterfactual Self

## 5. Mandatory Principles

- reflection must target structured evidence, not just language
- counterfactual analysis must be traceable
- reflective outputs are proposals, not direct authority
- self-critique must remain governed and auditable
- reflection must improve future behavior or model quality
