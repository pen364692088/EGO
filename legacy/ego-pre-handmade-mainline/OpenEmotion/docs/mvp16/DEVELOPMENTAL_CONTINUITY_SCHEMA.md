# Developmental Continuity Schema

## 1. Top-Level Schema

The developmental continuity system should maintain at least:

- developmental_identity
- trajectory_history
- growth_events
- branch_records
- developmental_goals
- stability_markers
- expansion_candidates
- long_horizon_memory_links

## 2. developmental_identity

Represents the currently maintained developmental identity across time.

Fields may include:
- identity_version
- continuity_anchor
- protected invariants
- active developmental orientation

## 3. trajectory_history

Represents the major transitions across long-horizon development.

Each entry should include:
- timestamp
- phase
- change_summary
- causal_basis
- supporting evidence
- replay reference

## 4. growth_events

Represents meaningful development events.

Examples:
- new stable tendency formed
- persistent conflict resolved
- maintenance architecture improved
- reflective policy matured

## 5. branch_records

Represents alternative developmental branches under evaluation.

Each branch should include:
- branch_id
- parent_reference
- divergence_reason
- expected benefits
- risks
- reversibility

## 6. developmental_goals

Represents durable long-horizon goals shaping future evolution.

## 7. stability_markers

Represents indicators of whether development remains coherent.

## 8. expansion_candidates

Represents possible future expansions that remain under governance review.
